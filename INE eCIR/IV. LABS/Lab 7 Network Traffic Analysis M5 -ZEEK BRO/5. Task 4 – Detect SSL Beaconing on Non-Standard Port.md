## **Step 5 ‚Äì Correlate with Timestamps**

Compare timestamps between `conn.log` and `ssl.log`:

- The client communicates with the remote server **every 5 seconds**.
    
- Such **regular, periodic connections** are not typical for normal SSL traffic.
    
- This strongly indicates **beaconing behavior**.
    

---

## **Step 6 ‚Äì Conclusion**

The evidence suggests the following:

1. SSL traffic was detected on a **non-standard port (80)**.
    
2. Communication used **self-signed certificates**.
    
3. Connections occurred **at fixed intervals (every 5 seconds)**.
    

üëâ These are strong indicators of **malware beaconing** ‚Äî where an infected host regularly ‚Äúcalls home‚Äù to a Command & Control (C2) server for instructions.
